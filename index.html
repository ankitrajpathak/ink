Help & Documentation — INK
What is INK?
INK is a SERP-first content engine built for the Zeron workspace. It crawls your brand, monitors your ICP’s trending topics, analyses the Google algorithm, and generates 5 fully optimised blogs every morning — ready to publish without editing.
Every blog is structured to rank #1 on Google by default. INK applies SEO, AEO (Answer Engine Optimisation), and GEO (Generative Engine Optimisation) layers so your content surfaces in traditional search, AI Overviews, and tools like ChatGPT and Perplexity.
Getting started
How do I set up my workspace?
Click the splash screen to begin onboarding. Enter your brand name, website URL, and LinkedIn company page. INK will crawl all three to build your content DNA — including tone, keywords, products, and positioning. This takes approximately 30 seconds.
How do I get my 5 daily blogs?
Once autonomous mode is enabled in Settings, INK generates 5 blogs every morning at 04:00 IST. They appear in Today’s Queue ready to publish. You can preview each one and select your publishing platforms.
How do I write a blog on a specific topic?
Go to Write on Demand in the sidebar. Type your topic or choose a quick suggestion, complete the 4-step wizard (audience, intent, format, platform), then click "Run Research & SERP Scan". INK fetches live data and generates a fully structured, rank-optimised blog in under 60 seconds.
Competitor Intelligence
INK’s Competitor Intel page uses Claude AI with live web search to analyse your competitors’ content strategies in real time. Add up to 5 competitor domains, click Analyse, and INK will fetch their blog frequency, content themes, keyword focus, and positioning gaps — using genuine web data, not estimates.
Is the competitor data real or simulated?
Real. INK sends each competitor domain to the Claude AI API with web search enabled. Claude fetches current content from the competitor’s website and social presence, then analyses it against your brand positioning to surface genuine strategic gaps and opportunities.
How long does competitor analysis take?
Each competitor takes 15–30 seconds depending on the size of their content footprint. Analysis runs in parallel for all added competitors once you click Analyse.
Understanding SEO / AEO / GEO scores
What is the SERP score?
The SERP score (0–100) rates how well a blog is optimised to rank on Google. It considers keyword density, heading structure, E-E-A-T signals, content depth, FAQ schema, and long-form requirements. 85+ is considered high-performing.
What is the AEO score?
AEO (Answer Engine Optimisation) rates eligibility for Google’s AI Overviews and SGE. It considers structured FAQ blocks, cited sources, clear answer formatting, and author attribution. A score above 80 means your blog is likely to surface as a source in AI answers.
What is the GEO score?
GEO (Generative Engine Optimisation) rates how likely your content is to be cited by ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, and other LLMs. It considers factual density, expert attribution, structured data, and brand entity consistency.
Algorithm Tracker
INK monitors Google, Bing, and SGE algorithm updates in real time using trusted sources: Search Engine Land, Search Engine Journal, and Google Search Central. When an update is detected, INK automatically adjusts its blog structure templates and notifies you via the bell icon in the topbar.
